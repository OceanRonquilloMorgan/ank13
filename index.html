FAQ

The 2018 National Defense Authorization Act (NDAA) rules governing the acquisition of commercial items should give those making relatively small purchases more choices, and allowing small businesses to participate in Federal expenditures.

Since President Donald Trump signed the 2018 NDAA into law on Dec. 12 2017, the focus has been on rules providing a three-fold increase in the micro-purchase threshold for civilian agencies from $3,000 to $10,000. A micro-purchase, because of its relatively low value, doesn’t require competition nor a contract. With few limitations, it allows contracting officers to purchase any product from any company, so long as it doesn’t exceed the $10,000 threshold.
